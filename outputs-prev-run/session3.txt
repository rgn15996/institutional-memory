## Gloucester Air / DXC OASIS — Pursuit Summary

---

### The Customer

**Gloucester Air** is a UK regional carrier: 62 aircraft, ~4,800 staff, hubs at Gloucestershire and Manchester, ~11m passengers/year and growing 9% YoY following the acquisition of two smaller route networks. That acquisition left the IT estate fragmented — the defining fact of the whole deal.

Key systems:
- **ServiceNow** — ITSM, 18 months in, heavily customised. The CIO's flagship project.
- **Dynatrace** — observability for core apps.
- **Nagios** — legacy ground-ops systems. *This is the blind spot that caused the July meltdown.*
- **ROSTA** — 15-year-old crew scheduling system. Only 2 engineers understand it. Epicentre of the incident.
- **Sabre** — reservations. Desynced from ROSTA during the incident.
- **SITA** — ground handling messaging. Ground ops worked from stale SITA data during the incident.
- ~60% on-prem across 2 DCs; AWS for customer-facing web/mobile.
- 24/7 NOC outsourced to **Calder IT Services** — contract expires March 2027.
- ~14,000 tickets/month; ~70% estimated repetitive/automatable.
- Deal size: **£6–8m over 3 years**.

---

### The July 5th Meltdown — The Defining Event

Everything changed around **4–6 July 2026**. A Nagios blind spot let a storage fault in ROSTA go undetected for 9 hours. Crew assignments desynced from Sabre; ground handling worked from stale SITA data. Result: **312 cancelled flights, ~£11m cost, front-page press coverage.** The board ordered an operational resilience programme with budget released *this quarter*. The old, relaxed timeline — October pilot, August pitch, March 2027 decision — **is dead**. Decision now wanted within two weeks of 2026-07-16; deployment starts **September 2026**, Phase 1 scoped to ROSTA + Nagios domain.
