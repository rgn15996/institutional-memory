# Pitch Strategy — DXC OASIS for Gloucester Air

*Agreed at pursuit review, 2026-06-12. SYNTHETIC DEMO DATA.*

## Deal thesis

Gloucester Air's ops estate is fragmented (ServiceNow + Dynatrace + Nagios +
Sabre + SITA + a 15-year-old crew system) and their NOC is outsourced to an
underperforming MSP whose contract ends March 2027. DXC OASIS is the agentic
operations layer that unifies it without replacing anything — automation at
scale on the repetitive ticket load, one real-time view across the estate,
predictive insight for Flight Ops.

## Recommended pitch sequence

1. **Open with the single-pane-of-glass demo** — every system, signal and
   decision in one unified view. This is what hooked Priya.
2. **Automation at scale** — agents on the 70% repetitive NOC tickets;
   humans freed for higher-value work.
3. **Predictive insight for Flight Ops** — surface IT risk before it becomes
   passenger disruption (Douglas's hot button).
4. **DXC heritage** — 60 years running mission-critical estates, airlines
   specifically; 7m+ devices under management. "You can't orchestrate what
   you don't operate."
5. **Close on a scoped pilot**: OASIS over the ground-ops estate
   (Nagios + SITA domain) for one quarter, starting **October 2026**.

## What to avoid

- Pricing detail — defer until the Priya business case is complete.
- Any language implying ServiceNow or Dynatrace get replaced (triggers O1).
- Overclaiming autonomy near Douglas until the compliance evidence pack
  exists (triggers O2).
