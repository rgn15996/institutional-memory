# DXC OASIS — Field Update for Pursuit Teams, 2026-07-18

*From OASIS product management. Supersedes prior competitive guidance.
SYNTHETIC DEMO DATA.*

## New in the July release — use these in-flight

1. **Agent Action Ledger.** Every agent action now produces a signed,
   immutable audit record: what the agent saw, what it proposed, who (or
   what policy) approved, what it did, and rollback state. Exportable as an
   evidence pack for regulated-industry review. *This closes the
   aviation-compliance evidence gap flagged by several pursuits.*

2. **Tiered autonomy policies.** Per-system guardrails: observe-only →
   propose (human approves) → act-with-rollback. Critical systems can be
   pinned to propose-only permanently. Position as "human judgement stays
   in command of anything that touches the operation."

3. **ROI Ledger dashboard.** Live, auditable tracking of hours returned,
   incidents predicted vs. occurred, and MTTR delta — mapped to a CFO-ready
   quarterly view. Early adopters report payback demonstrated in 2 quarters.

## Competitive guidance — Meridian AIOps

Meridian is appearing in UK/EU deals, usually introduced by incumbent MSPs
defending a renewal. Their claims and our counters:

- **"30% cheaper":** they price the software layer only. Their deployments
  require the incumbent MSP's managed service on top — total cost typically
  lands higher by year 2. Always reframe to 3-year total cost of operation.
- **"Deployed in 6 weeks":** true only for observe-only mode on cloud-native
  estates. No credible references on mixed legacy/on-prem estates
  (mainframe-era or bespoke ops systems). Ask them to name one.
- **"Full autonomy, no humans needed":** in regulated operations this is a
  liability, not a feature. Lead with the Action Ledger + tiered autonomy —
  ungoverned automation is what causes meltdowns, not what prevents them.
