*Urgent readout from the pursuit lead. Circulate to anyone touching this

## 1. The July 5th meltdown changed everything

Over the July 4–6 weekend Gloucester Air had a cascading IT failure: a Nagios
blind spot let a storage fault in the ROSTA crew-scheduling system go
unnoticed for 9 hours, crew assignments desynced from Sabre, and ground
handling worked from stale SITA data. **312 cancelled flights, ~£11m cost,
front-page coverage.** The board has ordered an "operational resilience
programme" with budget released **this quarter** — they will not wait for the
March 2027 Calder renewal.

Everything in our June strategy assumed an October pilot and an
August pitch. That timeline is dead. They want a decision **within two weeks**
and deployment starting September.

## 3. New objection — competitor in the room

Marcus disclosed they are also evaluating **Meridian AIOps** (backed by
Calder, the incumbent MSP). Meridian's pitch: 30% cheaper, deployed in
6 weeks, "full autonomy, no humans needed."

Douglas Petty pushed back on Meridian's full-autonomy claim after the
meltdown — he now sees ungoverned automation as the *risk*, not the fix.
His words: "The last thing I want is a black box restarting ROSTA."

## 4. What Marcus asked us for

A revised proposal that leads with **resilience and incident prevention**
(not ticket automation), a **phased deployment starting with the systems
that failed in July**, and a **quarter-by-quarter ROI model** Selina can
audit. He was explicit: "Don't show me a single pane of glass. Show me the
weekend of July 5th not happening."
